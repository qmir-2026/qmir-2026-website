# Task: Create Week 11 Homework Assignment — README.md

## Context

You are creating a file for **QMIR 2026**, a Bayesian quantitative methods course
for BA students in International Relations and European Politics at the University
of Mannheim. Strict conventions apply — read every constraint below before writing
a single line.

---

## Hard Constraints

### Language and Framing
- **Associational framing throughout** — no causal language anywhere in
  student-facing text. Write "is associated with", "predicts", "is linked to" —
  never "causes", "effect", "impact", "drives", or any causal verb.
- All math in **LaTeX only** — no Unicode math symbols (no →, ×, β as plain text).
- Course language is **English**.
- Spelling: **ressources** (French, intentional course convention) if the word
  appears.

### Formatting
- This is a **README.md** — use standard GitHub Markdown.
- **No callout boxes** (these are slide-only; Markdown files do not render them).
- **No code hints, no code templates, no example R snippets** in student-facing
  steps. Students are expected to apply the workflow independently.
- Each step: **one header + one concise instruction sentence**. No bullet
  sub-lists elaborating what the student should do within a step. No scaffolding.
- Do not add steps, sub-steps, or content beyond what is specified below.

### Pedagogical conventions
- **Minimal scaffolding**: headers and one instruction sentence per step only.
- **Three-phase structure**: Pre-Model → Model → Post-Model (label these as
  top-level sections containing the steps listed below).
- The homework follows the canonical 8-step Bayesian workflow taught in the
  course. Map the steps to the three phases as follows:

  **Pre-Model**
  - Step 1: DAG and Estimand
  - Step 2: Data Summary
  - Step 3: Formal Model Specification and Prior Justification

  **Model**
  - Step 4: Prior Predictive Check
  - Step 5: Fit the Models
  - Step 6a: MCMC Diagnostics
  - Step 6b: Posterior Predictive Check

  **Post-Model**
  - Step 7: Interpretation
  - Step 8: Limitations

## Theoretical Background and Hypotheses

Include the following theoretical framing verbatim (it is pre-approved):

> The 2016 Brexit referendum was as much a vote about national identity as it was
> about trade or sovereignty. Voters with a strong sense of British identity may
> have experienced EU membership as a constraint on national self-determination,
> making Leave the more emotionally and politically consistent choice.
>
> **H1:** Stronger British identity is associated with a higher probability of
> voting Leave.
>
> A large body of research suggests that the gender gap in nationalist attitudes
> is not uniform: men tend to score higher on measures of national attachment and
> are more likely to translate identity into exclusionary political preferences.
> If British identity operates differently for men and women, its association with
> Leave voting may vary by gender.
>
> **H2:** The positive association between British identity and the probability of
> voting Leave is stronger among men than among women.

## Models

Students fit **two models** using `brms` with `family = bernoulli(link = "probit")`:

- **Model 1 (Additive):** `LeaveVote ~ BritishIdentity + EuropeanIdentity +
  edlevel + age + hhincome + female`
- **Model 2 (Interaction):** same formula with `BritishIdentity * female`
  replacing the two additive terms

Priors for both models:
- Intercept: $\text{Normal}(0, 1)$
- All slopes: $\text{Normal}(0, 0.5)$

(These are the probit-scale analogues of the logit priors used in the Week 11
lecture. Do not explain the analogy in the README — just state the priors.)

## Step-by-Step Instructions

Write one header and one instruction sentence per step. No sub-bullets.
No code. No elaboration beyond what is written here.

**Pre-Model**

1. **DAG and Estimand** — Draw a DAG identifying the outcome, main predictor,
   and adjustment variables, and state a precise estimand for each hypothesis.
2. **Data Summary** — Visualise and describe the outcome variable and all
   predictors you plan to include in your models.
3. **Formal Model Specification and Prior Justification** — Write out the full
   statistical model (outcome distribution, linear predictor, link function,
   and all priors) for both models before writing any `brms` code, and justify
   your prior choices.

**Model**

4. **Prior Predictive Check** — Use `sample_prior = "only"` to verify that
   your priors produce plausible distributions of `LeaveVote` before
   conditioning on the data.
5. **Fit the Models** — Fit both models using `brms` with the probit link and
   the priors you specified in Step 3.
6a. **MCMC Diagnostics** — Assess convergence for both models using trace plots,
    $\hat{R}$, and bulk ESS.
6b. **Posterior Predictive Check** — Evaluate model adequacy for both models
    using `pp_check()` with an appropriate check type for binary outcomes.

**Post-Model**

7. **Interpretation** — Report posterior coefficient summaries, plot predicted
   probabilities to assess H1 and H2, compare the two models using LOO, and
   provide a substantive written assessment of each hypothesis given the data
   and the model comparison result.
8. **Limitations** — Identify at least two limitations of your analysis that
   could affect the credibility of your conclusions.
